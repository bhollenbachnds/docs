
The **Bluefox Digital Signage Content Feed** provider allows **real-time content integration** with **Bluefox**, a company specializing in **digital signage feeds**.  

---

## **Steps to Connect a Bluefox Content Feed**  

### **1. Start the Data Provider Wizard**  
- Follow the steps in **Adding a Data Provider** or **Editing a Data Provider**.  
- Select **Bluefox** as the provider type.  

### **2. Configure Bluefox Connection Settings**  
- Enter the **Bluefox Feed URL** to be used.  
- Click **Preview** to view the feed output as a **data table**.  

### **3. Retrieve the Feed from Your Bluefox Account**  
- Click **Open Bluefox Content Selector** to open the **Bluefox website**.  
- On the **feeds page**, select the **feed type** and **size**.  
- Copy the **feed URL** and paste it into the **Bluefox feed field** in PADS4.  

### **4. (Optional) Configure a Proxy Server**  
- If a **proxy** is required, enter the **proxy details**.  

### **5. Complete the Wizard**  
- Click **Next** to continue.  
- Finish the wizard as described in **Adding a Data Provider**.  

---

## **Important Considerations**  

- **If Using PADS4 Data Server:**  
  - Ensure the **Bluefox feed** is **accessible** from the **PADS4 Data Server's network**.  
  - The **Data Server** will **retrieve the feed** and distribute it to all connected **PADS4 Viewers**.  

- **If Not Using PADS4 Data Server:**  
  - Ensure that **each PADS4 Viewer** and **PADS4 Designer** has direct access to the **Bluefox feed**.  

---

## **Troubleshooting**  

- **The feed is not loading?**  
  - Check the **feed URL** for accuracy.  
  - Ensure your **internet connection** is active.  
  - If using a **proxy**, verify the **proxy settings**.  

- **Preview shows no data?**  
  - Make sure the **feed is active** in your **Bluefox account**.  
  - Try refreshing the **feed URL** on the Bluefox website.  

---

Once set up, the **Bluefox content feed** will automatically update, ensuring **dynamic and engaging content** for your **digital signage display**.
